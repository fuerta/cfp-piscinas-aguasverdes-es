Aguas Verdes: Charco de los Peñascos

Fuerteventura's best kept secret

Scroll to explore

Where the Atlantic meets volcanic rock

On the west coast of Fuerteventura, far from conventional tourist routes, lies a natural treasure of extraordinary beauty. The Natural Pools of Aguas Verdes, also known as Charco de los Peñascos, represent one of the most fascinating geological phenomena of the Canary Islands archipelago.

Formed over thousands of years by the relentless action of the Atlantic Ocean on volcanic basalt rock, these natural pools offer a visual spectacle that defies any description. The contrast between the deep black of the basalt and the green and turquoise-toned waters creates a landscape that seems from another planet.

The name "Aguas Verdes" (Green Waters) is no coincidence. The particular mineral composition of the volcanic rocks, combined with the presence of microscopic algae and the reflection of sunlight on the shallow waters, produces that characteristic emerald coloration that has captivated travelers and photographers from around the world.

This natural enclave is part of the protected landscape of Fuerteventura's west coast, where the island shows its wildest and most authentic face. Here, time seems to stand still, and visitors can experience the primordial connection between sea, land, and sky that defines the very essence of the Canary Islands.

Essential Information

Before visiting Aguas Verdes, it is essential to know these safety aspects

The Tides

Visiting the natural pools is only safe during low tide. Check the tide tables before planning your visit and never underestimate the power of the Atlantic Ocean.

Only visit at low tide
Access

The path to the pools runs over extremely uneven and sharp volcanic basalt rock terrain. Sturdy mountain footwear is absolutely essential.

Sharp basalt terrain
Safety

Atlantic waves can be extremely dangerous and unpredictable. Waves can reach the pools without warning. Always maintain constant vigilance of the sea.

Dangerous Atlantic swell
